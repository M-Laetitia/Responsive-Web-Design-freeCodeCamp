Margaret Burbidge

The stargazer who unveiled the secrets of the cosmos

Here's a time line of Margaret Burbidge's life:

  • 1919 - Born on August 12th in Davenport, England
  • 1936 - Begins studying astronomy at University College London
  • 1943 - Earns her Ph.D. from University College London
  • 1950 - Becomes the first woman to work as a scientist at the University of Chicago's Yerkes Observatory
  • 1955 - Becomes the first woman to give a plenary lecture at an American Astronomical Society meeting
  • 1957 - Joins the faculty at the University of California, San Diego
  • 1964 - Becomes the first woman to serve as president of the American Astronomical Society
  • 1971 - Becomes the first female director of the Royal Observatory, Greenwich
  • 1972 - Receives the National Medal of Science from President Nixon
  • 1976 - Awarded the National Medal of Science
  • 1977 - Becomes the first female director of the Royal Observatory in Greenwich
  • 1981 - Becomes the first female president of the American Astronomical Society
  • 1982 - Receives the Henry Norris Russell Lectureship from the American Astronomical Society
  • 2003 - Awarded the Bruce Medal by the Astronomical Society of the Pacific
  • 2005 - Receives the Bruce Medal for lifetime achievement in astronomy
  • 2011 - Awarded the Shaw Prize in Astronomy
  • 2012 - Awarded the Royal Astronomical Society's Gold Medal
  • 2018 - Delivers the keynote address at the American Astronomical Society meeting in Washington, D.C
  • 2020 - Dies at the age of 100.

"Margaret Burbidge is one of the giants in our field. She has contributed so much to our understanding of stars and galaxies, and the nucleosynthesis of the elements. Her work is an inspiration to all of us who study the universe."

-- Sandra Faber
